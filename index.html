Privacy Policy

Looma Interviewer AI Chrome Extension

Last Updated: January 7, 2025

1. Introduction

Looma Interviewer AI ("we", "our", "the Extension") is a Chrome browser extension designed to help recruiters and hiring managers conduct and analyze video interviews on Google Meet. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your data.

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Interview Content Data

When you use the Extension to record an interview, we collect:

  • Audio recordings of all interview participants
  • Video recordings of interview participants (if video is enabled)
  • Live transcriptions of spoken dialogue during the meeting
  • Meeting metadata: date, time, meeting ID, participant names

2.2 Technical Data

  • User preferences: recording quality settings, language preferences
  • Authentication tokens: to connect with your backend server
  • Extension usage data: button clicks, feature usage (stored locally only)

2.3 Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do NOT collect browsing history outside of Google Meet
  • We do NOT collect data from other websites or Chrome tabs
  • We do NOT use cookies or tracking pixels
  • We do NOT sell or share your data with third parties for advertising

3. How We Use Your Data

3.1 Primary Purpose

All collected data is used solely for:

  • Interview evaluation: Analyzing candidate responses and performance
  • Transcript generation: Converting speech to text for review
  • Recording storage: Saving interview sessions for later review
  • AI analysis: Processing interviews for insights and candidate assessment

3.2 Data Processing Location

  • All interview recordings are sent directly to your configured backend server
  • The Extension does NOT store interview content (audio, video, transcripts) in browser storage
  • Processing happens on your infrastructure - we do not host or access your data

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Storage Location

  • Browser Storage: Only stores user preferences, authentication tokens, and meeting metadata
  • Backend Server: All interview recordings and transcripts are stored on your self-hosted backend
  • No Cloud Storage: We do not maintain any cloud servers or databases that store your interview data

4.2 Data Retention

  • Data retention policies are controlled by your backend system
  • You have full control over how long recordings are stored
  • You can delete recordings at any time through your backend interface

4.3 Security Measures

  • All data transmission uses HTTPS encryption
  • Authentication tokens are stored securely in Chrome's storage API
  • No sensitive data is logged to browser console in production builds

5. Third-Party Services

5.1 Google Meet Integration

  • The Extension integrates with Google Meet to capture meeting content
  • We do NOT send any data to Google (except standard Meet functionality)
  • Google's own Privacy Policy applies to Google Meet usage

5.2 Your Backend Server

  • The Extension communicates exclusively with your configured backend server
  • We do not control or access your backend infrastructure
  • Your backend's privacy policy applies to data stored there

5.3 No Third-Party Analytics

  • We do NOT use Google Analytics or similar tracking services
  • We do NOT integrate with advertising networks
  • We do NOT share data with third-party AI providers

6. User Rights and Controls

6.1 Consent Requirements

  • You MUST obtain consent from all interview participants before recording
  • Recording without consent may violate laws in your jurisdiction
  • The Extension displays a recording indicator during active sessions

6.2 Data Access and Deletion

  • Access: You can access all your data through your backend system
  • Export: You can download recordings and transcripts at any time
  • Deletion: You can delete data through your backend interface
  • Uninstall: Removing the Extension deletes all local browser data

6.3 GDPR Compliance (EU Users)

If you are in the European Union, you have additional rights:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a portable format
  • Right to Object: Object to certain data processing activities

7. Permissions Explanation

7.1 Required Chrome Permissions

  • activeTab: To interact with Google Meet pages
  • storage: To save user preferences and authentication tokens
  • tabCapture: To record meeting audio and video
  • desktopCapture: Fallback method for screen sharing capture
  • meet.google.com: To access Google Meet interface

7.2 Why We Need These Permissions

Each permission is strictly necessary for core functionality. See the "Permissions" section in the Chrome Web Store listing for detailed explanations.

8. Data Protection for Organizations

8.1 Enterprise Usage

  • Organizations can self-host the backend for full data control
  • IT administrators can configure centralized backend URLs
  • All data remains within the organization's infrastructure

8.2 Compliance Certifications

  • The Extension architecture supports GDPR, CCPA, and SOC 2 compliance
  • Compliance responsibility lies with the organization operating the backend
  • We provide technical documentation for security audits

9. Children's Privacy

The Extension is designed for business use (recruitment and hiring) and is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from minors.

10. International Data Transfers
